## 2024-05-18 - [Safe #nosec suppression for huggingface_hub snapshot_download]
**Vulnerability:** Unsafe Hugging Face Hub download without revision pinning in `snapshot_download()` was reported by Bandit (`B615`).
**Learning:** This warning occurs when `snapshot_download` is called without `revision=` parameter, which is a potential supply chain vulnerability. However, in our specific case, the `snapshot_download` call was configured with `local_files_only=True`. With this parameter set, `snapshot_download` behaves strictly as a local cache check/retrieval mechanism and does not make any network requests. Therefore, there's no remote fetching occurring, and the absence of a `revision` parameter cannot lead to downloading tampered remote files. The warning from Bandit is a false positive in this specific execution path.
**Prevention:** It is safe to suppress Bandit warnings (e.g., `# nosec B615`) only when actions are guaranteed to be safe, such as calling `snapshot_download` with `local_files_only=True` where no remote fetching occurs. Always ensure that the suppression strictly applies to a context where the underlying risk is physically impossible to trigger.
